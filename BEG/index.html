The Route from Konya to Nevşehir

This part of study is focused on the Seljukid caravanserais between the cities Konya, Aksaray and Nevşehir.

Konya

The Capital of Anatolian Seljuks

Previous Next
Hocacihan / Dibidelikhan / Akyokuş / Hoca Cihan

Today in 2020, it is a fish restaurant called "Güven Balık Restaurant". It has only a covered section without a courtyard. The interior space is divided into 3 areas with 10 pillars as a two rows (Kılcı and Günel, 2013).

Previous Next
Horozluhan / Orozlu / Ruzbe Han / Ruz Apa / Ruz-apa Hanı

Horozluhan is located 6km northwest of Konya (Özergin, 1965). It has a classic plan type with covered section and a courtyard. It is built on the Ruzbe Plain where the sultans entering Konya were welcomed, or the sultans going out from Konya were sent off. It consists of a rectangular plan with five horizontal shafts and a courtyard in the west. The middle part of the covered section is separated from the side sections with a platform. The foundations found during the excavations in the courtyard were raised to a certain level and strengthened (Acun, 2007).

Previous Next
Zazadin Hanı / Sadeddin Köpek Han / Sa'deddin-köpek Hanı

It is located approximately 20-22 km east of Konya in Kavacık near Tömek village (Özergin, 1965; Ilter, 1969). It is the 9th khan from Aksaray to Konya (Özergin, 1965). The building has a big courtyard. In the covered section, the naves are cut with a vertical arch on the axis. The dome sits on the octagonal frame in the middle of the covered section. The door to the courtyard is in the southwest corner. There is a prayer room on the main gate (crown-shaped gate). The courtyard forms two ledges in the north direction, one deeper than the other. There are rooms on the north, south, and east sides of the courtyard, which have pointed arches, vaulted tops, open sides facing the courtyard, and animal porches. In the middle of the courtyard's west wall, there is the main gate of the covered section. Eight corners in the south-east corner of the caravanserai and some of the round wall piers in the south have square sections. The khan has two epigraphs, one on the outer entrance and the other on the inner portal. The one outside is fragmented, and the one inside is in better shape (Acun, 2007).

Zincirli Han / Han-i Zincirlü

It is 34km away from Konya, located in Zincirli village, approximately 10km from Akbaşhanı. In the resources by Ibni-Bibi, it is mentioned that there is a khan named Han-i Zencirlü, which could be Zincirli Khan. It is the 8th khan from Aksaray to Konya (Özergin, 1965). Although it is in ruins today, it is told that it has a courtyard (Ilter, 1969).

Previous Next
Akbaşhanı / Akbaş Hanı / Kaymaz Han

The Akbaş Khan is located 16km away from the Katrancı Khan and north side of the mountain of Sasa (Özergin, 1965). According to the sources it is the 7th khan from Aksaray to Konya (Özergin, 1965). It has a classic plan type with covered section and courtyard. It is seen that the traces left from the ruined building today consist of a covered section with three naves and a courtyard (Acun, 2007). Today, we can see the pointed vault beginnings and a wall part of the covered section. The dimensions of the covered section are 17.50 x 18m.

Previous Next
Zivarıkhan / Zıvarık Hanı / Züverek Hanı / Süverek Hanı

It is 25km away from Konya, and the 2nd khan from Konya to Ankara (Özergin, 1965). It has only a covered section, no courtyard.

Previous Next
Zulmanda Han / Zalmanda Hanı / Dinek Hanı

It is located on the north of the Obruk Khan, 3rd khan from Konya to Ankara (Özergin, 1965). Zulmanda Khan is consisted only a covered section, has no courtyard.

Previous Next
Katrancı Han

It is located 14km from the Obruk Khan in the Katranci village (Ilter, 1969). The exact place of the khan is unknown. But there are some ruins in the area near the village mosque. It is the 6th khan from Aksaray to Konya (Özergin, 1965).

Previous Next
Obruk Han

Obruk Khan is located 75km east of Konya, in the Obruk village near the Obruk Lake (Ilter, 1969). It is approximately 10-12km away from Okla Khan (Özergin, 1965). It is the 5th khan from Aksaray to Konya (Özergin, 1965). The building consists of a rectangular planned covered section with eight naves in the east-west direction and a square courtyard in the west. The naves of the closed section are cut with a vertical nave on the axis. There is a single row of arcades in the north and south of the courtyard and a two-story entrance section in the west. On the lower floor, there is a room on both sides of the entrance iwan, one with a door opening to the courtyard and the other to the gallery. On the upper floor, the room on the right is the prayer room of Obruk Khan with its altar. Next to the prayer room, there are interconnected rooms. The covered section of Obruk Khan is mostly ruined (Acun, 2007).
